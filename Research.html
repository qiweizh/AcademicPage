2 Research

Potential Interest Research

  1. Magma chamber dynamics, partial melting of mantle, evolution of volcanoes by means of experimental petrology and thermodynamic modeling.
  2. Transition of igneous and hydrothermal processes through the analysis of melt and fluid inclusions.

What I have done?

  1. Petrogenesis of high-silica granite. I found two groups of zircon in high silica granite with distinct geochemical compositions and then developed a zircon REE reserve program by MATLAB® (Section 2.3.1) to quantitatively calculation the percentage of co-precipitating minerals. The calculation shows a two-stage in-situ fractionation process during the formation of high silica granite. This result has been published in Chemical Geology.

  2. Tin deposits are closely associated with high-silica granite. I have collected a series of granite samples with different fractionation phase as well as tin-rich ore samples. The preliminary study suggests only slight enrichment of tin after magmatic fractionation. And the fluid inclusion study shows that the sanity of fluid increases with decreasing temperature, indicating a boiling event of hydrothermal fluid, which might be the main reason for the tin enrichment.

  3. The study of experimental geology requires students with a solid foundation in phase equilibria. I have finished reading the book ‘Petrologic Phase Equilibria’ by W.G. Ernst and read many phase diagram tutorial materials on the website. During the study, I found very difficult in understanding the ternary phase diagram due to my poor visualization skill. In order to improve that, I learned the 3D software 3D max® and AutoCAD® by myself and built 3D ternary models for the ternary eutectic system and An-Ab-Or system (Section 2.2).

  4. Geochemical researches often require great amount of work comparing data to those in literature. It is necessary and significant to find a quick way to create figures to show their geochemical variation. Because most scholars use Microsoft Excel to process data, I design two VBA® programs (SortData and QuickPlot) (Section 2.3.2) which incorporates the most common geochemical discrimination figures, trace element spider diagrams, Haker diagrams and etc.

What I am planning to do:

  1. Accumulate lab experience in experimental petrology.
  2. Since the METLS is open source on GitLab®, I have forked the program and tried to understand the code and update it if possible.
  3. Measure the composition of melt and fluid inclusions in my sample and wish to unravel the transition of igneous and hydrothermal processes.
