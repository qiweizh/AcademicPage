2.2.4 An-Or-Ab system

An-Ab-Or system

The binary phase diagram between An and Ab is a continuous solid solution system, between An and Or is eutectic system and between Ab and Or is exsolution system (Fig. 10).

Fig. 10 the 2D shape of An-Ab-Or system

When they form a ternary phase diagram, it contains four phase regions (a) Homogeneous liquid phase: L (b) L+ plagioclase (c) one solid phase, plagioclase (with minor Or), alkali feldspar (with minor An) (d) miscibility gap. The boundary between (a) and (b) is liquidus surface. It has three subsurfaces which meet at eutectic point (Fig.11 a). The boundary between (b) and (c) is an irregular twisted surface which has a face contact with the miscibility gap (Fig. 11b). The miscibility gap is an irregular 3D object, which could be produced by the ‘loft’ as discussed before (Fig. 11c).

Fig. 11 3D model of An-Ab-Or system

After building the 3D phase model, the isothermal sections can be obtained through the 'get section' function in the software (Fig. 12). They show a continuous change of each phase region, proving the credibility of our model.

Fig. 12 isothermal sections of An-Ab-Or system

After drawing the isothermal sections, it is easy to determine the composition and amount of each phase. For example, suppose overall liquid composition is X, with the temperature lowering, the liquid first begin to crystallize An rich feldspar P, and the fraction of liquid is PX/PQ, the fraction of solid B is XQ/PQ govern by Inverse Lever Rule (Fig. 13a). As the temperature continues lowering, feldspar begins to dissolve into two phases. The point P, J, Q construct phase triangle, and the fraction of feldspar P is XN/PN, the fraction of feldspar Q is KX/KQ, the fraction of liquid is MX/MJ.
